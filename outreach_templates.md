## Template 1: Referral Request (1st-degree connection at company)

**Gold standard reference:** James Chiu message — BrainStation opener, specific relevant experience, explicit ask at the end.

---

### Message

Hey [Name],

Hope you're doing well. I noticed [Company] is hiring a [Role] — [one specific thing about the role or team that makes it genuinely interesting, e.g., "the AI personalization work looks right up my alley"].

Quick bit of context on me: I've spent the last [X years] building AI/ML products — most recently [most relevant proof point, e.g., "I shipped a $2.2M ARR unlock at Hearth in 3 days using Claude Code for data synthesis"]. [Second proof point if relevant, e.g., "Before that I led the ML recommendation engine at Guestlogix across 6 airline partners."] It maps pretty well to what they're looking for.

Would you be comfortable putting in a word or referring me? Happy to send a blurb you can forward, or just make it easy — whatever works for you.

Thanks either way.
Hirsch

---

### Notes

**When to use:** You have a 1st-degree LinkedIn or IRL connection at the company. They don't need to know the hiring manager directly — just being inside the company and willing to refer is enough.

**What to customize:**
- `[Name]` — their first name
- `[Company]` — company name
- `[Role]` — exact job title
- Sentence 2: something real and specific about the role, team, or product (check the job description or company blog)
- First proof point: pick the one most relevant to the role (fintech = Hearth or neobank; AI = Hearth/Deloitte chatbot; enterprise = Deloitte $13M; 0→1 = Sci-Insights or neobank)
- Second proof point: optional, only include if it directly connects to the role

**Lead proof point by role type:**
- Head of Product / VP Product → $23M revenue impact across 8 engagements
- AI/ML product → $2.2M ARR in 3 days (Hearth, Claude Code)
- Fintech → neobank in 4 months, 50K MAUs
- Enterprise / audit / B2B → Deloitte $13M, 8 consecutive board milestones

**Voice check:** No em dashes. Short sentences. Real ask at the end (not "let me know if you'd be open"). Implicit flattery ("you're well-connected there") is banned.

## Template 2: Warm Outreach (2nd-degree or cold, no direct connection)

---

### Message

Hi [Name],

I came across [Company]'s work on [specific thing — product, feature, fundraise, blog post, or hiring angle] and it caught my attention. [One sentence on why it's genuinely interesting or relevant.]

I'm a senior AI PM with [X years] building data-heavy products — [most relevant proof point, e.g., "I took a neobank from 0 to 50K MAUs in 4 months"]. [Second line connecting to their space, e.g., "Most of my recent work has been at the intersection of AI and fintech, which is why [Company]'s approach to [X] stood out."]

I noticed you're hiring for [Role]. I'd love to learn more about the team and what you're solving. Is there a good way to connect?

Hirsch

---

### Notes

**When to use:** 2nd-degree connection, cold outreach to a PM leader, hiring manager, or someone on the product team. You found their name on LinkedIn or through a job posting. No mutual connection to lean on.

**What to customize:**
- `[Name]` — their name (usually first name is fine)
- `[Company]` + specific thing: do 2 minutes of research. A recent blog post, product launch, fundraise, or feature mention goes a long way. Don't make this up.
- First proof point: match to their space (fintech → neobank; AI → Hearth; analytics/data → 50GB+ genomic analysis or Claude Code work)
- Second connecting line: map your background to what they're building, not generically ("I like fintech") but specifically ("I've worked with similar data constraints in B2B2C fintech products")
- `[Role]` — exact job title

**Lead proof point by role type:** Same as Template 1.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't open with "I'm impressed by what you're building at [Company]" — it's hollow
- Don't ask for advice as a disguise for a job ask — be direct
- Don't send this to a recruiter; this is for PMs, product leaders, or hiring managers

**Voice check:** Ends with a real question. The ask is specific (learn more, connect) but not aggressive (not "can we schedule a call this week"). Short. No em dashes.

## Template 3: Intro Request (mutual connection)

---

### Message

Hey [Name],

Quick ask — I see you're connected to [Target Name] at [Company]. They're hiring a [Role] and I think it's worth a conversation. I'm a senior AI PM — most recently at Hearth (Series B fintech), before that Deloitte Ventures and an EF-backed AI startup. My background maps well to what they're building.

Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to send a blurb you can forward.

Thanks either way.
Hirsch

---

### Notes

**When to use:** You don't have a direct connection at the company, but someone in your network does. They know someone there — but they're not at the company themselves.

**What to customize:**
- `[Mutual Name]` — your connection's first name
- `[Target Name]` — the person at the company you want to reach
- `[Company]` — the company name
- `[Role]` — exact job title

**Voice check:** No "hope you're well", no filler, direct ask, "thanks either way" closes it.

**Note:** This is the hardest message to send — keep it short and make the ask explicit. You're asking someone to spend social capital on your behalf. Respect their time: one sentence of context, one direct ask, easy out.

## Template 4: Recruiter Response (recruiter reached out first)

---

### Message

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out. Yes, I'm interested — [Company]'s [specific thing, e.g., "work on expense management infrastructure" or "Series C momentum"] caught my eye recently.

Quick context: I'm a senior AI PM, most recently at Hearth (fintech, Series B). Before that I ran a fractional AI PM studio with $23M in revenue impact across 8 engagements. I tend to work best in roles with real product scope — owning strategy, roadmap, and a team.

Happy to connect for a screen and learn more about the role. [One scheduling question, e.g., "Does [Day/time] work, or what does availability look like on your end?"]

Hirsch

---

### Notes

**When to use:** A recruiter or talent partner messaged you first (LinkedIn InMail, email, or via referral like Ramp). You're interested and want to confirm it without sounding desperate or generic.

**What to customize:**
- `[Name]` — recruiter's first name
- `[Company]` + specific thing: one real detail that shows you know who they are (check their LinkedIn, press, or the job posting)
- Scheduling line: if you have a specific availability, use it. If not, throw it back to them with a question.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't over-explain your background — they'll ask in the screen
- Don't hedge ("I might be open to exploring opportunities") — you're either interested or you're not
- Don't mention salary/comp expectations until they bring it up

**Signals this template sends:**
- You're senior (Head of Product / $23M impact), not a mid-level candidate
- You have scope expectations (strategy + team), which screens out roles that are too junior
- You're warm but not chasing them

**Voice check:** Short. Two paragraphs max. Ends with a direct scheduling question. No corporate filler.
